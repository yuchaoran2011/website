Intro

Chaoran Yu (于超然) is a software engineer working on big data problems. Trained at UC Berkeley Department of EECS, he's been working at Silicon Valley and New York since 2014. While not coding, you can find him hiking in the woods of the Sierra Nevada, or watching lava gush into the sea at the Big Island. He is also an avid reader who's eager to learn anything about the world that he does not understand. Of special interests to him are history, finance and psychology.

Work

Chaoran is passionate about distributed systems for data processing and storage. Open source softwares are his favorite. Over the years, he has worked with and accumulated extensive experience in Spark, Kafka and Cassandra, to name a few. This field is rapidly evolving. Spark achieved a milestone by beating MapReduce to become the first mainstream in-memory data processing engine. Then there comes Flink with native support for processing unbounded datasets. With the advent of Aapche Beam, engineers are pushing the boundaries of data-intensive systems ever further. Chaoran is happily involved in this movement, hoping to contribute to and take advantage of these efforts to build applications that are resilient, responsive and scalable.
